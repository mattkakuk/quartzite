# **What Is Quartzite? An Introduction to This Natural Marvel**

Quartzite is a stunning natural stone formed over thousands of years through
the intense heat and pressure of geological processes. It’s often mistaken for
marble but is actually much harder and more resilient. Why is this important?
Because durability matters when choosing a work surface in your kitchen.

## **Quartzite vs. Quartz: What’s the Difference?**

_While their names are similar, quartzite and quartz are quite different._  
- **Quartzite** is natural, mined directly from the earth, then polished for use as countertops.  
- **Quartz** is engineered, a mix of crushed quartz and resin.

Choosing quartzite means opting for a 100% natural product — each slab is one
of a kind, like a fingerprint.

# **Maintaining Your Quartzite Countertops**

### **Daily Cleaning Tips**

Use a soft cloth with mild soap and water. Avoid acidic cleaners like vinegar
or lemon juice, which can etch the surface.

### **Sealing Your Quartzite**

A high-quality sealant is recommended every 1-2 years to prevent staining and
protect the stone’s natural beauty.

### **Dealing with Spills and Stains**

Promptly wipe up spills, especially oil or wine. If stains appear, specialized
cleaning products are available that won’t damage your surface.
