## **What Is Quartzite, Really?**

Quartzite is a natural stone formed when sandstone is transformed under heat
and pressure. That process creates a dense, hard surface—often harder than
granite—while keeping an elegant, natural appearance.

### **Quartzite vs. Quartz—Don’t Mix These Up**

Quartz (engineered) is man-made. Quartzite (natural) is mined from the earth.
If you want one-of-a-kind patterns, organic movement, and a slab that nobody
else has, quartzite is the real deal.

### **Quartzite vs. Marble—The “Looks Like Marble” Advantage**

Quartzite often gives you marble-like veining without the same level of
softness. It’s a smart pick for homeowners who love that luxury look but also
cook, host, and live in their kitchens.

## **Granite and Quartzite—Which One Fits Your Kitchen?**

Here’s the truth: both stones are great, but they shine in different ways.

### **When Granite Is the Right Choice**

Granite is classic, durable, and often offers a wide range of speckled
patterns and deep tones. It’s a reliable choice for traditional and
transitional kitchens.

### **When Quartzite Is the Better Move**

Quartzite is ideal when you want natural drama—flowing veining, lighter
palettes, and a more “designer” look. It’s especially popular in modern Fort
Lauderdale renovations.
